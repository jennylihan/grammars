This project was inspired by James Russell's 1980 paper titled "A Circumplex Model of Affect". In it, he describes a model for mapping emotions onto a 2-dimensional coordinate plane with affect on the x-axis (positive ← → negative) and valence on the y-axis (high ← → low energy). Emotions are not discrete.


His model is incomplete. For one, emotion words vary by language and culture. For another, how is it impossible to quantify qualitative data? I wanted to explore both of these aspects in an interactive art piece.


The idea is to simulate swimming through the affective space. One thing you'll notice is you have to move around to discover the emotions. Another thing is that it's difficult to see the emotions when you're next to them; you see emotions most clearly for what they are when you're feeling the opposite.
